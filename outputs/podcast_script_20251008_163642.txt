Eve: Precisely! Synchronous communication really hinders fault tolerance. Plus, things like event propagation for notifications become a bottleneck when you rely on direct calls.

Adam: Okay, so we've established the problems. That's where asynchronous, event-driven communication steps in, right? It's like the superhero swooping in to save the day.

Eve: Kafka persists events, giving you at-least-once delivery guarantees. You might get a duplicate message occasionally, but you won't lose any. And there are dead letter queues for failed messages.

Adam: Okay, so we're talking about fault tolerance, scalability, and reliability. It sounds like a much more robust system.
